## Learnings & Insights

### What Worked Exceptionally Well

1. **Booking Pattern Reuse**
   - Core booking flow identical across Restaurant, Salon, Gym
   - Only unique elements varied by vertical
   - Massive time savings (1-2 hours per template)

2. **Tone Differentiation**
   - Restaurant: Warm & hospitable
   - Salon: Personal & pampering
   - Gym: Energetic & motivating
   - Each tone perfectly matches customer expectations

3. **South African Context Integration**
   - Load shedding mentions where relevant
   - Hair texture diversity in salon
   - Halal/kosher options in restaurant
   - Makes templates feel localized, not generic

4. **Special Occasion Handling**
   - Restaurant: Birthdays, anniversaries, proposals
   - Salon: Weddings, photoshoots, events
   - Gym: Fitness transformations, New Year goals
   - Creates emotional connection

5. **Dynamic Template Seeding**
   - Automatically detects all JSON files
   - Update-or-insert prevents duplicates
   - Scales perfectly to 20+ templates

### Challenges Encountered

1. **Balancing Flexibility vs Specificity**
   - Too flexible: Bot gives vague responses
   - Too specific: Bot sounds robotic
   - **Solution:** 200-250 word system prompts with examples

2. **Hair Type Sensitivity**
   - Important cultural consideration in South Africa
   - Needed to mention expertise without stereotyping
   - **Solution:** List all texture types equally, emphasize expertise

3. **Membership vs Session Booking**
   - Gym has both memberships (long-term) and class bookings (short-term)
   - Two different conversation flows needed
   - **Solution:** Separate intents with clear triggers

4. **Large Group Reservations**
   - Restaurants need special handling for 8+ people
   - Bot can't guarantee availability
   - **Solution:** Collect details, mention staff will confirm, handoff

5. **Trial/Tour Requests**
   - Gym needs to offer trials without committing availability
   - Balance between welcoming and realistic
   - **Solution:** Offer day pass, collect info, schedule with staff confirmation

### Improvements for Future Templates

1. **Intent Library Creation**
   - Extract common intents into shared library
   - Reduce duplication across templates
   - Maintain consistency automatically
   - **Planned for Week 7**

2. **Rule Template System**
   - Create rule category templates
   - Privacy rules, tone rules, escalation rules
   - Copy-paste for new templates
   - **Planned for Week 7**

3. **Variable Naming Standards**
   - Document standard variable names
   - business_name, operating_hours, location, contact_number
   - Consistency improves code readability
   - **Documentation updated**

4. **South African Localization Checklist**
   - Load shedding mention
   - Rand currency (R)
   - SA phone formats
   - Cultural sensitivity
   - Local context
   - **Added to quality checklist**
